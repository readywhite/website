# Ready White SEO Launch Checklist

Use this checklist after publishing the Squarespace marketing layer or routing the public domain. It protects speed-to-lead, clean indexing, recurring property-manager acquisition, and standardized interior-only positioning.

## Squarespace SEO source of truth

- Site title: `Ready White | Fast Interior Property Refreshes`
- Site description: `Ready White provides fast interior repaint and property refresh services for rentals, apartments, property managers, investors, and move-out turnovers in Central Pennsylvania.`
- Title format: `Page Name | Ready White`
- URL slugs: `/`, `/services`, `/vendors`, `/locations`, `/gallery`, `/get-started`, `/contact`

## Post-publish checklist

- Verify site in Google Search Console.
- Request Google indexing for the homepage and core pages.
- Verify Bing Webmaster Tools.
- Connect Google Analytics.
- Confirm SSL is enabled.
- Confirm the custom domain is connected.
- Submit `https://www.readywhite.com/sitemap.xml`.
- Review search keywords monthly.

## SEO report readiness

Check these items during every SEO review:

- missing alt text on before/after and property refresh images.
- Missing SEO descriptions or duplicate page descriptions.
- Broken internal links between Home, Services, Vendors, Locations, Gallery, Get Started, and Contact.
- Page titles that do not follow `Page Name | Ready White` except the site title homepage.
- Image sizes over 500 KB or total page size over 5 MB.
- Random URL slugs or pages published outside the approved slug list.
- More than one H1 on any page.
- Missing Open Graph title, description, image, type, or URL tags.

## Operational SEO notes

- Keep Ready White positioned as an interior-only property turnover operating system, not a traditional painting company.
- Use local terms naturally: apartment painting Harrisburg PA, rental repaint Harrisburg, property manager painting Central PA, apartment turnover repainting Pennsylvania, and move-out painting services.
- Prioritize pages that increase quote starts, property manager relationships, vendor capacity, review flywheels, and stale-lead recovery opportunities.
