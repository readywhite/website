# Ready White Room Pricing Model

Ready White pricing should be package-based and margin-protected. Do not use flat hourly labor as the default customer-facing pricing model or vendor payout model.

## Baseline Room Economics

Minimum room charge: `$1,200`

Standard target price: `$2,500`

Average room reference:
- 400 square feet
- 6 labor hours

Reference costs:
- Labor: `$500`
- Paint: `$70`
- Overhead allocation: `$30`

Minimum all-in cost: `$600`

Target gross profit: `$600-$1,900` per room

## Pricing Principles

Use:
- package-based quotes
- standard size bands
- standard add-ons
- photo-based scope verification
- exception escalation for non-standard prep

Protect:
- gross margin
- vendor fulfillment reliability
- quote speed
- scope clarity
- repeatability for property managers

## Standard Pricing Logic

Every estimate should classify:
1. property type
2. vacancy status
3. room count
4. room size band
5. wall condition
6. ceiling condition
7. trim/door inclusion
8. stain/smoke/water damage risk
9. prep severity
10. required timeline

The minimum room charge is the floor unless an approved package, promotion, or bundled property-manager agreement explicitly overrides it.

## Exception Pricing

Hourly or change-order pricing is only appropriate for:
- severe prep
- water damage
- smoke or stain blocking
- custom repairs
- approved scope exceptions
- missing or inaccurate photo information that changes the verified scope

Exception pricing should be separated from the base package so the customer and vendor both understand what changed.
