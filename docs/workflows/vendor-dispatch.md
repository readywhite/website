# Vendor Dispatch Workflow

Vendor dispatch converts an approved Ready White scope into a standardized subcontractor work order with clear buy rates, photo-proof requirements, and callback accountability.

## Dispatch Preconditions

Do not dispatch until all are true:

- Opportunity is in **Vendor Assignment** or later.
- Customer quote is approved.
- Scope is package-based or exception work is explicitly approved.
- Vendor buy rate is attached to the dispatch record.
- Required photos and address details are available.
- Schedule window is confirmed or ready for vendor confirmation.

## Dispatch Steps

1. Select vendor from the approved vendor bench.
2. Confirm package buy rate, add-ons, and exception items.
3. Send vendor the standardized scope, address, access notes, due date, and photo proof checklist.
4. Require vendor response by SLA before moving to **Scheduled**.
5. Move to **Scheduled** only after vendor confirms availability.
6. Move to **In Progress** when work starts.
7. Collect before/during/after photo proof.
8. Move to **Photo Proof Review** when vendor submits completion proof.
9. Approve completion or request correction.
10. Move to **Completed**, then **Review Requested**, then **Closed Won** when billing/review loop is complete.

## Vendor Response SLA

- ASAP/vacant turnover jobs: vendor acceptance target under 30 minutes.
- Standard jobs: vendor acceptance target same business day.
- No response by SLA: reassign or activate backup vendor.

## Vendor Dispatch Message Must Include

- Job address.
- Contact/access instructions.
- Package name and room bands.
- Included surfaces and excluded surfaces.
- Add-ons and approved exceptions.
- Buy rate and completion deadline.
- Required photo proof list.
- Callback/correction expectations.

## Photo Proof Requirements

Vendor must submit:

- Before photos matching customer scope areas when needed.
- After wide photo of each completed room.
- After close-up of the prior worst wall or repaired area.
- Photos of ceilings/trim when included.
- Photos of stain-blocking, patching, or exception work before paint coverage when applicable.

## Operational Redundancy

Maintain backup vendors by market and package type. If a vendor misses response SLAs, quality requirements, or proof standards, reduce assignment priority until scorecard review is complete.
