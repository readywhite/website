# Vendor Onboarding SOP

Vendor OS execution consistency is now the highest ROI operational layer after estimation stabilization. Vendors are execution nodes in Ready White's logistics network, not random subcontractors.

## Activation checklist

A vendor cannot receive production assignments until the checklist in `config/vendor-onboarding-checklist.json` is complete:

- scope standards acknowledged,
- package buy rates acknowledged,
- photo proof training completed,
- response SLA acknowledged,
- callback policy acknowledged,
- completion rules acknowledged,
- insurance and tax docs collected,
- test job or reference review completed.

## Response SLAs

| Event | SLA |
| --- | ---: |
| New assignment acknowledgement | 15 minutes |
| Schedule confirmation | 60 minutes |
| Proof upload after completion | 30 minutes |

## Scorecard fields

Track every active vendor by:

- on-time rate,
- callback rate,
- proof compliance rate,
- estimate variance,
- response speed,
- QA failure rate,
- customer satisfaction.

## Completion rules

No vendor job can move through completion without before photos, after photos, wall ID mapping, timestamped checklist, and exception notes for prep damage or scope change.

## ROI / operational impact / scalability / risk reduction

- **ROI impact:** better vendors reduce callbacks, margin leakage, and PM churn.
- **Operational impact:** onboarding becomes a repeatable gate instead of founder judgment.
- **Scalability impact:** each market can add capacity without inventing local standards.
- **Risk reduction:** vendors cannot enter dispatch without proof, SLA, and callback expectations.
