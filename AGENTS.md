# Ready White Operating Constitution

Ready White is an AI-native property-turnover operating platform, not a traditional painting website. Treat this repository as the operational memory for the business.

## Operating priorities

Optimize every change for:

- speed-to-lead
- operational reliability
- recurring property-management relationships
- standardized workflows
- reduced vacancy turnover time
- pipeline integrity
- fast execution
- scalable automation

## Architecture guardrails

Preserve the current early-stage stack unless there is a clear operational reason to change it:

- Squarespace = marketing layer
- Railway = backend orchestration layer
- GoHighLevel = CRM + automation system
- Vendor Ops = subcontractor fulfillment network

Do not redesign the stack for vanity features. Protect deterministic scripts, stable naming conventions, standardized GoHighLevel objects, operational documentation, environment-variable security, and production-safe architecture.

## Business model guardrails

Ready White sells standardized property-turnover execution through subcontractor fulfillment. Favor package-based quoting, photo-based scope verification, vendor standardization, recurring property-manager relationships, operational speed, and automation reliability.

Never build the operating model around flat hourly labor. Use preset package buy rates, standard size bands, standard add-ons, and exception escalation workflows. Hourly/change-order rates are only for severe prep, water damage, smoke/stain blocking, custom repairs, and approved scope exceptions.

## Required GoHighLevel standards

Pipeline name: `Ready White Customer Jobs`

Pipeline stages, in order:

1. `New Lead`
2. `Photos Requested`
3. `Photos Received`
4. `Scope Review`
5. `Quote Sent`
6. `Follow-Up`
7. `Approved`
8. `Vendor Assignment`
9. `Scheduled`
10. `In Progress`
11. `Photo Proof Review`
12. `Completed`
13. `Review Requested`
14. `Closed Won`
15. `Closed Lost`

Standard tag examples:

- `source:squarespace`
- `vertical:property-management`
- `vertical:investor`
- `timeline:asap`
- `vacant:true`

- `lead:quoted`

## Photo policy

Require quote photos that reduce scope drift and protect margins:

- 1 wide photo of each room
- 1 photo of the worst wall in each room
- photos of ceilings, trim, stains, peeling paint, water damage, smoke damage, holes, and heavy prep areas

Use photos to detect exception jobs before quoting or dispatching.

## Change requirements

When implementing workflow or automation changes, update the relevant operational docs, SOPs, outreach YAML, audit scripts, KPI reporting notes, and GoHighLevel object standards. If a workflow can be standardized, automate it. If a workflow creates operational drift, constrain it.
