# Estimating Documentation

This folder defines Ready White's AI-assisted estimating standards. Estimating should maximize quote speed while using customer photos to prevent scope drift and protect margins.

## Estimating goals

- Turn qualified photo submissions into fast, standardized quotes.
- Detect exception jobs before approval.
- Preserve package-based pricing and avoid custom hourly estimating.
- Give vendors clear, dispatch-ready scopes.
- Reduce vacancy turnover time for property managers and investors.

## Required customer photo policy

Before quote finalization, require:

- 1 wide photo of each room.
- 1 photo of the worst wall in each room.
- Photos of ceilings, trim, stains, peeling paint, water damage, smoke damage, holes, and heavy prep areas when present.

Use photos to:

- Confirm room count and approximate size band.
- Identify add-ons and exceptions.
- Prevent scope drift.
- Protect margin.
- Preserve quote speed.

## AI-assisted estimate flow

1. Lead enters GHL from Squarespace or another approved source.
2. GHL requests missing photos if the submission is incomplete.
3. Estimator reviews photos for room count, size band, paintable surfaces, add-ons, and exceptions.
4. AI can assist with checklist extraction, photo completeness review, exception detection, and quote draft preparation.
5. Human operator approves the quote before customer delivery.
6. Approved quote moves through the GHL pipeline using standardized stages and tags.

## Exception triggers

Escalate before final quote if photos show:

- Severe prep.
- Water damage.
- Smoke damage.
- Heavy staining.
- Peeling paint.
- Large holes or failed repairs.
- Occupied-property complexity.
- Access constraints.
- Ambiguous room count or missing room photos.
