# Room Pricing Standards

Ready White prices work as a standardized property-turnover operating system, not as an open-ended hourly painting service. Pricing must protect speed-to-lead, margin consistency, vendor clarity, and repeatable property-management relationships.

## Operating Principles

- Quote from package bands first; escalate only true exceptions.
- Keep room definitions deterministic so intake, estimating, dispatch, and vendor proof use the same scope language.
- Use photos to confirm scope, prevent drift, and preserve quote speed.
- Avoid custom pricing unless the job contains severe prep, water damage, smoke/stain blocking, custom repairs, or another approved exception.
- Keep every package tied to a matching vendor buy rate before quoting customer-facing pricing.

## Standard Room Size Bands

| Band | Typical room type | Scope boundary | Estimating note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Small | Bathroom, laundry, small office, small bedroom | Standard walls under normal prep | Use when photos show limited wall area and no major repairs. |
| Medium | Bedroom, dining room, standard living room | Standard walls under normal prep | Default band for most turn rooms. |
| Large | Large living room, primary suite, open common area | Larger wall area or higher wall count | Confirm wall count and ceiling height from photos. |
| Oversized / open-plan | Great room, combined kitchen/living, commercial-like area | Non-standard wall area, height, or access | Requires estimator review before quote. |

## Base Packages

| Package | Included | Excluded / add-on triggers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Walls-only turnover | Standard wall repaint, normal patch/sand touch-ups, one standard color family | Ceilings, trim, doors, cabinets, severe prep, stain blocking, water damage, smoke damage. |
| Walls + ceiling refresh | Walls-only scope plus standard ceiling repaint | Ceiling stains, popcorn repair, water damage, specialty coatings. |
| Walls + trim reset | Walls-only scope plus standard baseboards/casings | Door slabs, railings, heavy enamel prep, damaged trim replacement. |
| Full room reset | Walls, ceiling, and trim in standard condition | Severe prep, custom repairs, smoke/stain blocking, specialty finishes. |

## Standard Add-Ons

- Door painting.
- Accent wall color change.
- Additional color beyond standard package rules.
- Minor drywall patch beyond normal nail holes.
- Ceiling stain spot prime when isolated and photo-confirmed.
- Baseboard/trim refresh when not included in the selected package.
- Stairwell or high-access area review.

## Exception Escalation

Escalate out of package pricing when photos or intake identify:

- Severe prep or peeling paint.
- Water damage or active moisture indicators.
- Smoke damage, nicotine staining, or heavy odor history.
- Large holes, texture matching, or custom drywall repair.
- Cabinet painting, specialty finishes, exterior work, or commercial access constraints.
- Occupied-unit logistics that materially slow production.

Exception work may use approved hourly/change-order rates only after scope review. Do not let exception rates become the default quoting method.

## Margin Protection Rules

1. Confirm photos before final quote whenever the customer has not provided minimum photo coverage.
2. Keep customer package price, vendor buy rate, and add-ons in the same scope record.
3. Never dispatch a vendor from notes alone; dispatch from the standardized scope and photo set.
4. Track callbacks by package, vendor, room type, and exception reason.
5. Review room package performance monthly and adjust package bands only when recurring data shows a margin or cycle-time issue.
